# Real Gold Properties — Website CMS Guide
### How to manage your website content (no tech knowledge needed)

---

## What is the CMS?

CMS stands for **Content Management System** — think of it as the "control panel" for your website. You log in, make changes, and they appear on your live site. You never need to touch any code.

**Your CMS address:** `yourdomain.com/cms/`  
**Login:** Use the username and password provided to you.

---

## Logging In

1. Go to your CMS address in any browser
2. Type your **Username** and **Password**
3. Tick **"Remember me"** if you want your username saved next time
4. Click **Sign in**

> If you forget your password, click "Forgotten your password?" below the sign-in button.

---

## SECTION 1 — Managing Pages

Your website has **7 main pages**. You edit them under **Pages** in the left menu.

Click **Pages** → you will see a tree/list. Click the page name you want to edit, then click the **Edit** (pencil) icon.

### How page editing works

Each page is built from **sections** stacked on top of each other — like building blocks. You can:
- **Edit** any section by clicking on it
- **Reorder** sections by dragging the handle (≡) up or down
- **Add** a new section using the big **+** button at the bottom
- **Remove** a section by clicking the bin icon on that section

Always click the big **Save draft** or **Publish** button at the top right when you are done.

> **Save draft** = saves your changes but does not go live yet.  
> **Publish** = your changes go live on the website immediately.

## SECTION 2 — Managing Properties

Click **Property Listings** in the left menu.

You will see a list of all your properties. You can **search**, **filter** by status or category, and click any property to edit it.

### Adding a new property

Click the **New** button (top right). Fill in the fields:

**Basic Info**
| Field | What to put |
|---|---|
| Title | Property name or address |
| Address | Street address |
| City / State / Postcode | Location details |
| Status | For Sale / For Rent / Sold / Pending |
| Category | for-sale / for-rent / sold |
| Featured | Tick this to show it in the marquee/showcase |
| Badge | Optional label (e.g. "New Listing") |

**Price**
| Field | What to put |
|---|---|
| Price | Number only (e.g. 850000) |
| Price Label | What to display (e.g. "$850,000" or "Price on application") |
| Sold Price | Only fill in if status is Sold |

**Property Stats**
| Field | What to put |
|---|---|
| Bedrooms / Bathrooms / Garages | Numbers |
| Area (sqft) / Lot Size | Floor area and land size |
| Year Built | Year |

**Content**
| Field | What to put |
|---|---|
| Overview | Short 1–2 sentence summary (shown on card) |
| Description | Full description for the property detail page (supports formatting) |
| Map embed URL | Google Maps embed link for the location map |
| Video tour URL | Link to a video tour (YouTube or direct) |

**Card Image** — the main photo shown on the listing card.

**Gallery Images** — click **Add image** to add multiple photos for the detail page.

**Features** — click **Add feature** to list features (e.g. Pool, Solar Panels). Each has an icon, title, and optional description.

**Detail Rows** — click **Add detail row** to add label/value pairs (e.g. "Land size" → "612m²").

**Nearby Locations** — click **Add location** to list nearby places (e.g. "Westfield · Shopping · 2.1 km").

Click **Save** when done.

---

### Changing property status

1. Find the property in the list
2. Click to open it
3. Change the **Status** field (For Sale → Sold, etc.)
4. Click **Save**

## SECTION 3 — Portfolio Showcase

Click **Portfolio Showcase** in the left menu.

This is a **curated selection** of properties shown in the homepage showcase section. It is separate from Property Listings — you hand-pick which ones appear here.

### Adding a showcase item

Click **New**. Fill in:

| Field | What to put |
|---|---|
| Title | Property name |
| Location | Suburb or area |
| Price | Display price |
| Status | e.g. "Sold" or "For Sale" |
| Beds / Baths / Area | Quick stats |
| Background Image | Large parallax photo (shown behind the card) |
| Thumbnail | Smaller card photo |
| Order | Lower number = shown first |
| Active | Tick to show, untick to hide |

> **Tip:** If the property is already in your Property Listings, type its slug in **Property Slug** so clicking the showcase card links to the full listing.

## SECTION 6 — Viewing Form Submissions

Click **Form Submissions** in the left menu. You will see three sub-items:

### Property Enquiries
Messages sent by people enquiring about a specific property. You can see the person's name, email, phone, which property they asked about, and their message.

### Contact Submissions
General messages sent through the contact page.

### EOI Submissions
Formal Expression of Interest offers submitted by buyers. These include full legal buyer details, offer price, deposit, finance conditions, and solicitor information.

> These are **read-only** — you can view and search submissions but not edit them. Use them to follow up with clients directly.

**Exporting:** On EOI Submissions and Property Enquiries, you can download a CSV spreadsheet (look for the Export button).

**Marking as read:** Click into a submission and tick **Is read** to mark it as handled.

## SECTION 7 — Syncing Properties from VaultRE

If your properties are managed in **VaultRE** (your property management system), the website can pull live data from it.

Click **Sync Properties** in the left menu, then click the **Sync Now** button.

This refreshes the property data from VaultRE. Do this after you update listings in VaultRE to keep the website in sync.

> You only need to do this when you want to update — it does not happen automatically.

## SECTION 8 — Managing Images

Click **Images** in the left menu.

Here you can see all uploaded photos. To upload new images, click **Add an image** and select a file from your computer.

> **Tip:** Images are also uploaded directly when you are editing a property, team member, or page section — you do not always need to come here first.

## Tips & Things to Remember

- Always click **Publish** after editing a page if you want it to go live straight away. **Save draft** keeps it saved but not visible on the website.
- You can **preview** your changes before publishing by clicking the **Preview** button (eye icon).
- If something looks wrong on the website after editing, check that you clicked **Publish** and not just **Save draft**.
- The **Order** field on team members, testimonials, and showcase items controls the display order. Lower number = appears first. Use 0 for the most important item.
- The **Active** tick on team members, testimonials, and showcase items is a quick on/off switch — untick to hide something without deleting it.
- If you are unsure about a change, use **Save draft** first and preview it before publishing.
